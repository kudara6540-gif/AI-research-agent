# Baseline Review: SYK Models & Baby Universe Implementations

**Date:** 2026-05-21
**Topic:** New developments in Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev (SYK) models for Baby Universe implementations

## Executive Summary
Recent research in quantum gravity and holography has focused on explicitly constructing "baby universes"—closed, disconnected spacetimes born from topological fluctuations—within the lower-dimensional, tractable framework of the Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev (SYK) model and its bulk dual, Jackiw-Teitelboim (JT) gravity. Since late 2024, researchers have transitioned from conceptual discussions to concrete mathematical operator constructions (e.g., in the double-scaled SYK model) and defining holographic entanglement measures for these isolated spacetimes.

## Key Developments (2024–2026)

### 1. Explicit Baby Universe Operators in Double-Scaled SYK
In **arXiv:2408.03726 (August 2024)**, Okuyama et al. introduced explicit baby universe operators $\mathcal{B}_a$ in the double-scaled SYK (DSSYK) model. 
- **Mechanism:** The operator creates a baby universe of size $a$ and is constructed using the transfer matrix $T$ on a chord Hilbert space.
- **Holographic Implication (ER=EPR):** The identity operator on this space can be expanded as a linear combination of baby universes. More importantly, the thermofield double (TFD) state of DSSYK is generated by a pair of these operators, representing a "double trumpet" wormhole geometry. This provides a highly concrete, exact matrix-model realization of the ER=EPR conjecture.

### 2. Baby Universes from Thermal Pure States
In **arXiv:2512.00149 (November 2025)**, Sasieta and Swingle presented a 2D holographic model showing closed baby universes emerging from thermal pure states in SYK.
- **Mechanism:** This utilizes a first-order thermal phase transition (the Maldacena-Qi phase transition in coupled SYK systems) and the insertion of a heavy matter operator to support the baby universe. 
- **Entanglement:** Below the transition temperature, the bulk entanglement entropy between the empty Anti-de Sitter (AdS) region and the baby universe is $O(N)$. This entanglement can be rigorously defined by coarse-graining over the random SYK couplings. 

## State of Implementations

While pure analytical results dominate the literature, numerical and code-level implementations are beginning to form:
- **Matrix Models:** The baby universe operator in DSSYK heavily relies on the ETH matrix model formulation, meaning exact diagonalization and sparse matrix methods (commonly used in SYK) can be directly applied to trace the creation of these topological states.
- **Thermofield Double (TFD) States:** Existing repositories (e.g., `vipasu/SYK-TFD` using Variational Quantum Circuits) provide the scaffolding. Since Okuyama et al. demonstrated that the DSSYK TFD state is equivalent to a pair of baby universe operators, near-term quantum simulations of SYK TFDs are effectively simulating the boundary dual of this baby universe "double trumpet". 
- Libraries like `QuSpin` (for Majorana fermions) and custom Julia/Python ED scripts are the primary tools used by authors to verify finite-$N$ behaviors.

## Open Questions & Future Watch Signals
- **Finite-$N$ vs Large-$N$:** How do finite-$N$ effects (which stabilize the ensemble average) alter the factorization problem typically associated with baby universes?
- **Quantum Simulation:** Can the baby universe operator $\mathcal{B}_a$ be efficiently compiled into a quantum circuit for simulation on near-term hardware?
- **Code Releases:** We are actively monitoring PapersWithCode and GitHub for explicit numerical solvers of the DSSYK baby universe operators.
