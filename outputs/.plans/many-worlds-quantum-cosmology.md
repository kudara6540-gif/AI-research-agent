# Research Watch Plan: Many-Worlds in Quantum Cosmology

## Objective
Monitor and synthesize real-time research papers that enhance the sophistication, theoretical grounding, and physical validity of the Many-Worlds Interpretation (MWI) (Everettian quantum mechanics), specifically within quantum mechanics and quantum cosmology.

## What to Monitor
- ArXiv repositories (primarily `quant-ph`, `gr-qc`, and `hep-th`).
- Peer-reviewed journals focusing on quantum foundations, gravity, and cosmology (e.g., Physical Review D, Foundations of Physics).
- Keywords: "Many-Worlds Interpretation", "Everettian", "quantum cosmology", "wave function of the universe", "decoherence in MWI", "probability in MWI".

## Signals That Matter
- **Theoretical advances:** New derivations of the Born rule, resolutions to the preferred basis problem.
- **Cosmological applications:** MWI applied to the Wheeler-DeWitt equation, multiverse cosmology, or early universe quantum fluctuations.
- **Experimental proposals:** Any proposed macroscopic quantum coherence tests or empirical distinctions (however slight) involving MWI.

## Meaningful Change
- A publication introducing a novel mathematical framework linking MWI to quantum gravity.
- A highly-discussed preprint resolving long-standing philosophical or mathematical critiques of MWI.
- New consensus or debate on MWI's role in the string theory landscape or cosmic inflation.

## Check Frequency
- **Weekly** automated scans for new preprints and published papers.
